Bug bounty programs are an excellent way for companies to pay for verifiable results to improve security. This is especially helpful due to the cybersecurity workforce shortage. I was surprised that price was not the primary factor for quantity or quality of reports. The fact that size and popularity do not exclude a company from benefiting makes the concept even more universally useful. More data is necessary to determine why certain industries get less attention. I am not certain the only reasons are related to the value of the leaked information exceeding the bounty. This would imply that even those employed to help companies are also stealing from them.
